EastWest Transpersonal Training

Focus: YMYL & E-E-A-T

Executive Summary

For a "Your Money Your Life" (YMYL) website in the mental health and education sector, trust is the primary currency. The strategy focuses on establishing high E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) to capture students at three critical stages of their decision-making process: Awareness, Consideration, and Action.

Keyword Strategy by Intent

Informational

Awareness

Users asking questions and seeking definitions. High volume, low conversion, builds trust.

  • "What is transpersonal psychotherapy?"
  • "Holotropic breathwork benefits and risks"
  • "Can breathwork heal trauma?"
  • "Difference between Grof Breathwork and Holotropic"

Commercial

Comparison

Users comparing schools and evaluating credibility. Medium volume, medium conversion.

  • "Best transpersonal psychology training Europe"
  • "EUROTAS accredited schools reviews"
  • "Online vs in-person breathwork certification"
  • "Grof breathwork facilitator cost"

Transactional

Action

Users ready to enroll or apply. Low volume, high value.

  • "Transpersonal psychotherapy certification 2026"
  • "EastWest transpersonal training application"
  • "Breathwork intensive modules Germany"
  • "Apply for holotropic training"

YMYL Compliance Checklist (Trust Factors)

Must-Have Pages

  • Contraindications Page: Explicit safety protocols for breathwork.
  • Teacher Profiles: Full academic credentials and years of experience for Kati Wortelkamp & Lyudmila Skartsesku.
  • Accreditation Logos: EUROTAS, GTT (if applicable), in the footer of every page.
  • Code of Ethics: A dedicated page outlining the ethical standards of the school.

Technical Trust Signals

  • HTTPS/SSL: Non-negotiable for secure application forms.
  • Local SEO: Google My Business profiles for the Germany and Moldova locations.
  • Testimonials: Real student reviews (video preferred) embedded on the homepage.

Content Roadmap Recommendations

1. The "Safety First" Hub

Create a dedicated section on "Safety in Altered States." This addresses the primary objection of potential students and satisfies Google's YMYL requirements.

2. Comparison Guides

Write an honest article: "University Psychology Degree vs. Transpersonal Training." Highlight the experiential gap that universities miss.

3. Alumni Success Stories

Interview past students. Focus keywords: "Career as a transpersonal therapist," "Integration work jobs."

4. Localized Landing Pages

Create specific pages for: "Transpersonal Ausbildung Deutschland" and equivalent for Moldova/Russia to capture local search traffic.
